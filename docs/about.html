Planning research

When planning and preparing for my research, I started by reflecting on my academic interests and the broader implications of understanding mental illness through a historical lens. The decision to explore ancient explanations and treatments for mental illness arose from my fascination with how different cultures shaped their understanding of mental health based on their philosophical, spiritual, and medical frameworks. I aimed to investigate how these historical perspectives influenced modern mental health practices and cultural attitudes. To narrow my focus, I selected four regions—Mesopotamia, Egypt, Greece, and China—because of their significant contributions to early medicine and philosophy. These regions provided rich and diverse examples of how ancient civilizations approached mental illness from both supernatural and rational perspectives. My selection process involved reviewing primary and secondary sources to identify civilizations with substantial documentation of medical and cultural practices related to mental health. Each region offered a unique lens through which to examine the interplay of cultural beliefs, medical theories, and treatment methods.
